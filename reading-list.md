# Reading List — IA Generativa e Media (A.A. 2025/2026)

Prof. Fabio Giglietto · DISCUI · Università degli Studi di Urbino Carlo Bo

## Mandatory Readings

### Week 1 (23–25 Feb) — Generative AI Fundamentals

1. Chadwick, A. (2011). *The hybrid media system* [Conference paper]. European Consortium for Political Research General Conference, Reykjavik, Iceland.

2. Ferrara, E. (2026). *The Generative AI Paradox: GenAI and the erosion of trust, the corrosion of information verification, and the demise of truth*. arXiv preprint arXiv:2601.00306v1.

3. Pew Research Center. (2025, September). *How Americans view AI and its impact on people and society*. Pew Research Center.

### Week 2 (2–4 Mar) — Production, Disinformation & Regulation

4. Gilardi, F., Di Lorenzo, S., Streiff, B., Ezzaini, J., Zurfluh, E., Santa, B., & Hoes, E. (2025). *Willingness to read AI-generated news is not driven by their perceived quality*. arXiv preprint arXiv:2409.03500v3.

5. Hameleers, M., & van der Meer, T. (2026). Beyond textual disinformation: Comparing the effects of textual disinformation to AI-generated and video-based visual disinformation across different issues. *New Media & Society*. https://doi.org/10.1177/14614448251409208

6. Vaccari, C., & Chadwick, A. (2020). Deepfakes and disinformation: Exploring the impact of synthetic political video on deception, uncertainty, and trust in news. *Social Media + Society*, *6*(1), 1–13. https://doi.org/10.1177/2056305120903408

### Week 4 (16–18 Mar) — Project Launch

7. Marino, G., & Giglietto, F. (2024). Integrating large language models in political discourse studies on social media: Challenges of validating an LLMs-in-the-loop pipeline. *Sociologica*, *18*(2), 87–107. https://doi.org/10.6092/issn.1971-8853/19524

8. Terenzi, M., & Giglietto, F. (2025). *From AI slop to strategic virality: The impact of synthetic visuals on public discourse in Italian Facebook communities* [Working paper].
