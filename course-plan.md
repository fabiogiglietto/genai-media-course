## Group Project: AI Slop Reception Analysis

### Project Overview

Students will work in groups of 4â€“6 to produce a research paper analyzing AI-generated content ("AI Slop") on Meta platforms, with a focus on user reception in comments. The project applies the LLMs-in-the-loop methodology to validate AI-assisted analysis against human coding.

### Research Focus

- Identifying and classifying AI-generated visual content (synthetic images, engagement bait)
- Analyzing user comments and reactions to AI Slop content
- Using Gemini to process large datasets of posts and comments
- Validating AI classifications through human coding comparison
- Connecting findings to platform policies and EU Code of Practice on Disinformation

### Methodology

Following Marino & Giglietto (2024), groups will develop LLMs-in-the-loop pipelines using Google Gemini to analyze content at scale, then validate results through systematic human coding. This dual approach addresses both the practical challenge of large datasets and the methodological need for reliability assessment.

## Google AI Tools: Course Platforms

All hands-on exercises will use Google Gemini and NotebookLM, available to all students through the university's partnership with Google. Access is provided via your @uniurb.it Google Workspace account.

### Google Gemini

Primary tool for content generation, analysis, and multimodal tasks.

- Text generation and conversation for content creation workflows
- Multimodal analysis (text, images, video, audio)
- Image generation with Imagen for visual content creation
- Long context window (up to 1M tokens) for analyzing large datasets
- Integration with Google Workspace (Docs, Sheets, Slides)
- Code execution capabilities for data analysis

### Google NotebookLM

AI-powered research assistant for document analysis and synthesis.

- Upload and analyze multiple documents (PDFs, Google Docs, web pages)
- AI-powered Q&A grounded in your sources with citations
- Audio Overview: generate podcast-style discussions of your materials
- Study guides, FAQs, and summaries generation
- Ideal for literature review and connecting theory to findings
- Collaborative notebooks for group research projects
